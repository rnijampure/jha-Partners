The Chaat Company

Anshu Dua, Founder of The Chaat Company, brings

Indian flavors to America.

In 2018, Anshu Dua realized his vision to turn flavours of India by launching his Whole Foods brand The Chaat Company in the US. His story is one that speaks of reaching to one’s ethnic roots…

Company:
The Chaat Company
Founders:
Anshu Dua , Founder & CEO, The Chaat Co., New York
Milestones:
Launched first product line in ~100 Whole Foods in 2016 Raised $1M in capital from angels and strategic investors Rebranded and re-launched The Chaat Company in 2018

For Anshu Dua , The Chaat Company has been an attempt to reconnect with his Indian heritage and its rich culture. After leaving his career in technology start-ups, Anshu built The Chaat & Co. - the first Indian street snacking CPG brand, bringing the flavors of Indian chaat culture to the American snack aisle.

After several rounds of angel investments with Jha partners and others, Chaat & Co. was able to roll out their Savory Yogurt Snack, a line of dome-style single-serve cups of whole milk probiotic savory yogurt with lentil puff topping. The Yogurt Snack was a first-of-its kind for the U.S. market, and became an instant success.

After spending two years in store demoing and researching the retail landscape, Anshu finally rebranded and re-launched an updated line of fresh, grab & go Indian street snacks in 2018. The Chaat Company launched its first product line in 2016 through a partnership with Whole Foods and other natural grocer retailers in the east coast. Today the company is working with a new strategic partner and investor in India for sourcing authentic ingredients to build a portfolio of grab & go Indian street snacks.

Anshu believes that Americans today have become more privy to new food experiences.

Millennials and Gen Zers are Chaat & Cos. biggest fans, as they are willing to explore new global cuisines and are looking for simple, healthy and fresh snacks.
